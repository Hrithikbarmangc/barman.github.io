This project covers the research published in my preprint, **arXiv:2510.15419 (2025)**. It explores the complex interplay between chirality and spatial confinement.

### Research Summary
In this work, I investigate the dynamics of **Chiral Active Brownian Particles (CABPs)** when they are restricted by external potentials or physical walls. The study identifies how confinement alters the typical transport properties of self-propelled agents.

### Key Highlights
* **Time-Reversal Symmetry:** Demonstrated that the combination of chirality and confinement leads to significant time-reversal symmetry breaking.
* **Delay Correlations:** Quantified the "delay" between a particle's internal orientation and its actual displacement vector under confined conditions.
* **Angular Velocity Effects:** Analyzed how the distribution of angular velocities influences the long-time diffusion and steady-state distributions of the particles.
